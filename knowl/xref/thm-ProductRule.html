Theorem 2.4.1. The Product Rule.

If \(u(x)\) and \(v(x)\) are functions that are differentiable at \(x\) and \(f(x) = u(x)\cdot v(x)\text{,}\) then:
\begin{equation*} \ddx{f(x)} = u'(x)\cdot v(x) + u(x) \cdot v'(x)\text{.} \end{equation*}
For convenience, this is often written as:
\begin{equation*} \ddx{u\cdot v} = u'v + uv' \hspace{1cm} \text{or}\hspace{1cm} \ddx{u\cdot v} = v \left(\dd{u}{x}\right) + u \left(\dd{v}{x}\right)\text{.} \end{equation*}
