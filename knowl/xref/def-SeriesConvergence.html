Definition 8.2.2. Series Convergence.

We say that the infinite series \(\sum_{k=1}^\infty a_k\) converges to the real number \(L\) if the sequence \(\{S_n\}_{n=1}^\infty\) converges to \(L\) (\(\lim_{n\to\infty} S_n = L\)), where \(S_n = \sum_{k=1}^n a_k\) is the \(n\)th partial sum of the infinite series.
If the sequence of partial sums \(\{S_n\}_{n=1}^\infty\) diverges (the limit \(\lim_{n\to\infty} S_n\) does not exist), then we say that the infinite series \(\sum_{k=1}^\infty a_k\) diverges.
