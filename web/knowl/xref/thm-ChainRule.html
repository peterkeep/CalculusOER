Theorem 2.5.2. The Chain Rule.

For the composite function \(y=f(g(x))\text{,}\) if we define \(u=g(x)\) and \(y=f(u)\text{,}\) then, as long as both \(f\) and \(g\) are differentiable at \(u\) and \(x\) respectively:
\begin{equation*} \ddx{f(g(x))} = \frac{d}{du}\left(f(u)\right)\cdot \frac{d}{dx}\left(g(x)\right)\text{.} \end{equation*}
Alternatively, this can be written as:
\begin{equation*} \dydx = \dd{y}{u} \cdot \dd{u}{x} \hspace{1cm} \text{or}\hspace{1cm} \frac{d}{dx}\left(f(g(x))\right) = f'(g(x))\cdot g'(x)\text{.} \end{equation*}
