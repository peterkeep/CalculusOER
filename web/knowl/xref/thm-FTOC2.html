Theorem 5.4.4. Fundamental Theorem of Calculus (Part 2).

For a function \(f(x)\) continuous on the closed interval \([a,b]\) and some \(F(x)\text{,}\) an antiderivative of \(f(x)\text{,}\) then
\begin{equation*} \int_{x=a}^{x=b} f(x)\;dx = F(x)\bigg{|}_{x=a}^{x=b} = F(b)-F(a)\text{.} \end{equation*}
The vertical bar means "evaluated," and \(F(x)\bigg{|}_{x=a}^{x=b}\) is typically read as "\(F(x)\) evaluated from \(x=a\) to \(x=b\text{.}\)"
