Definition 8.5.1. Alternating Series.

An infinite series \(\sum_{k=0}^\infty a_k\) is called an Alternating Series when \(a_k = (-1)^k|a_k|\) or \(a_k = (-1)^{k+1}|a_k|\) for all \(k=0,1,2,...\text{.}\) That is, the sign of the terms alternates:
\begin{equation*} \sum_{k=0}^\infty a_k = |a_0| - |a_1| + |a_2| - + ... \end{equation*}
